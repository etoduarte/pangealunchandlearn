```css

```

### Typography Treatments
- **Product/Interface heading**: "Send Money" with "How much do you want to send?" body text below
- **Style variants shown**: "This is a text" (regular), "Italic text" (italic), "Display" (black weight)
- **DISPLAY** label shown indicating the display category

### Key Typography Rules
1. Dark navy text on gradient backgrounds (strict rule — NOT white)
2. Tight line-height for display (~0.9) vs generous for body (~1.6)
3. Negative letter-spacing for display, standard for body
4. Uppercase only for overlines/section numbers/labels
5. Weight hierarchy: Black (900) → ExtraBold (800) → Bold (700) → Regular (400) — limited medium/semibold use

### Easing and Timing System

| Type | Duration | Easing | Use Cases |
|------|----------|--------|-----------|
| Micro | ~200ms | ease-out | Hover, focus, toggles, button press |
| Standard | ~350ms | cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1) | Page transitions, panels, fade in/out, slide |
| Entrance | ~600ms | cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) | Scroll-triggered reveals, section entrances |
| Hero | ~900ms | cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) | Hero entrances, large movements, page load |


### Motion Patterns

#### Scroll Reveal
- `opacity: 0 → 1`
- `translateY(30px → 0)`
- Stagger: 100ms between sequential elements

#### Parallax
- Decorative elements at 0.3-0.5x main scroll speed
- Creates depth without distraction

#### Float / Hover Animation
- Subtle vertical movement: up/down ~10px
- Duration: 3-4 seconds per cycle
- Infinite loop, ease-in-out
- Used on paper planes, coins, notifications

#### Gradient Shift
- Hero gradient subtly shifts hue on scroll
- Very subtle, adds life

## Critical Design Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Cream, not white**: Primary background is `#F5F0EB` (warm cream). White is for cards and reversed text only.

2. **Dark navy text on gradients**: Display/headline text on cyan-blue gradients MUST be dark navy (#002B49), never white. This is the brand's defining typographic choice.

3. **Halftone texture on ALL gradients**: Every gradient surface gets the dot texture overlay. No bare gradients.

4. **Paper plane as primary motif**: The 3D paper plane is the key recurring decorative element.

5. **Section numbering**: Every major section gets a two-digit number (01-09) in overline style.

6. **Footer bar is omnipresent**: Dark navy footer bar with logo, breadcrumb, and page number on every view.

7. **Warm + cool tension**: The brand identity lives in the contrast between warm cream and cool cyan.

8. **3D illustration style**: Smooth, rounded, glossy/metallic, primarily cyan with gold accents for monetary elements.

9. **Authentic diversity in photography**: Genuine diversity, authentic moments, not staged stock.

10. **OS-aware color behavior**: Dark mode follows OS accent color; light mode follows OS highlight color.

11. **Subtle brand presence on light backgrounds**: ψ pangea mark used subtly in headers/footers.

12. **Geometric consistency**: The ψ mark, paper plane, and location pin shapes share angular, directional geometry suggesting movement and destination.

## Key Deltas & Counterintuitive Findings

| Common Assumption | Actual Brand Rule |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Hero text is white on gradient | Dark navy bold on cyan gradient |
| Background is white | Warm cream `#F5F0EB` is primary canvas |
| Simple flat gradients | Gradients ALWAYS have halftone dot texture overlay |
| White is the card color everywhere | White on gradients, off-white (#FAFAF8) on cream |
| Section numbers are afterthoughts | First-class typographic element, large and prominent |
